Three views. Nothing else.

Daybreak is deliberately small. You get a week, you get a day, and you get a focus view for the task in front of you. No dashboards, no reports, no team layer, no AI.

Week

A kanban of seven days plus a "sometime" bucket. Drag tasks between days. See what's coming before it arrives.

Day

Today's tasks with timeboxes, pinned alongside your HEY calendar events. A daily log lives here too.

Focus

One task at a time. A quiet timer. A single place to work until the thing is done.

Basecamp integration

Your Basecamp work, already planned.

Sign in once with your Basecamp account. Daybreak pulls in your assignments and schedules, preserves project names, and lets you triage your Basecamp inbox straight into a day or a week. Rate-limited and cached so you stay under Basecamp's 50 req / 10s limit.

  • OAuth sign-in via Launchpad
  • Auto-sync assignments and schedules
  • Triage to today, a specific day, or "sometime"
  • Back-links to the original Basecamp card

HEY integration · optional

Your email and calendar, made plannable.

Connect HEY with a secure PKCE OAuth flow and Daybreak layers in calendar events, journal updates, and email triage (Imbox, Reply Later, and Set Aside) where you can turn a message into a task in one click. HEY is entirely optional; every feature has a local fallback.

  • Calendar events pinned to your day view
  • Triage Reply Later or Set Aside into planned work
  • HEY journal digest pushes to your reflections
  • No secret required, uses HEY's public PKCE client

A morning ritual. An evening ritual.

Planning is a practice, not a preference. Daybreak opens with a morning ritual to shape the day and closes with an evening one to log it.

Morning

Review yesterday's open items. Plan today: pull in assignments, add calendar events, set a single focus. The ritual marks your day as planned when you're done.

Evening

Close out open tasks: finish, defer, or drop. Reflect in a short journal prompt. The ritual logs the day's completion so tomorrow starts clean.

Self-host

Run it yourself in one command.

Daybreak is single-user and made to run on your own machine or a small server. No account to create, no usage to meter, no one watching what you plan.

git clone https://github.com/is2b007/daybreak.git
cd daybreak
cp .env.example .env     # add your Basecamp credentials
docker compose up -d
open http://localhost:3000

Your SQLite database and files live in ./storage on the host. Back up that folder, that's your whole data set.

Philosophy

Opinionated, not optional.

Daybreak was built to replace a $240-a-year planner with something calmer, more opinionated, and shaped around how Basecamp and HEY already work. It follows the 37signals stack: Rails 8, Hotwire, SQLite. No React. No Tailwind. No TypeScript. No bundler. No team features, no AI, no analytics, no notifications.

Software should have opinions. This one does. If you disagree with the opinions, the code is open. Fork it and build the planner you actually want.
